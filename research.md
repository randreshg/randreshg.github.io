# Research

My dissertation work centers on execution contracts: explicit declarations of State, Dependency, and Effect that make scheduling-relevant semantics compiler-visible across HPC and agentic AI systems.

In practice, I co-design:

- LLVM and MLIR compiler pipelines
- OpenMP and event-driven runtime systems
- Typed workflow IRs for agent orchestration
- Research infrastructure that improves reproducibility and developer productivity

## Themes

### Compilers

LLVM, MLIR, OpenMP lowering, optimization passes, execution-model translation, and typed intermediate representations.

### Runtime Systems

Task graphs, asynchronous overlap, event-driven scheduling, distributed execution, and heterogeneous placement.

### Agentic AI

Typed orchestration, graph execution, tool integration, workflow validation, and compiler-visible semantics for agent systems.

### Research Tooling

Benchmarking, experiment tracking, validation, and local-first infrastructure for technical teams.
